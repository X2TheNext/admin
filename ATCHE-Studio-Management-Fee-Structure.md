# ATCHÉ Studio: Talent Management & Fee Structure

## Role of ATCHÉ Studio

ATCHÉ Studio is the agency layer that sits between brands and Circle creators. When a brand wants UGC, ambassador content, or AI-likeness content, ATCHÉ Studio negotiates the deal, contracts both sides, manages delivery and quality control, and handles payment. Creators get access to vetted, paying brand work without doing their own sales, contracting, or invoicing; brands get a curated roster instead of sorting through open marketplaces. This is the same agency relationship already built into the UGC Content Creator Agreement and Brand Ambassador Agreement in `ATCHE-Studio-Contract-Templates.docx`.

## Commission Tiers

The baseline commission is 15-20%, consistent with the rate already used for booking fees and brand activations elsewhere in the business model. In practice this should be tiered by deal type and Studio's role in sourcing it:

Brand-sourced deals that ATCHÉ Studio originates and negotiates from scratch (cold outreach, pitch decks, ongoing brand relationships like Spicy Water) sit at the top of the range, 20%, because Studio is doing the sales work, not just the matchmaking.

Inbound deals, where a brand already knows the creator or comes through the creator's own network but routes the contract and payment through ATCHÉ for protection, sit at 15%, since Studio's role is contracting, QC, and payment processing rather than sales.

Recurring/retainer deals with a brand (monthly content packages, ongoing ambassador relationships) can run slightly lower, 12-15%, once the relationship is established and the sales effort is amortized — this rewards Studio for landing recurring revenue and gives the brand a reason to commit long-term.

AI likeness licensing royalties (revenue stream #9) are a separate line, not folded into the above. Per the addendum in the contract templates, the creator receives a per-use or royalty fee any time their likeness is reused in AI-generated content, additive to their original appearance fee. ATCHÉ Studio takes a smaller cut here — suggested 10% — since this is largely passive income for the creator and the marginal cost to Studio per reuse is low (no new shoot, minimal new negotiation).

## Payout Flow

The brand pays ATCHÉ Studio the full deal value, on the terms set in the Brand Ambassador or UGC Agreement (typically net-15 or net-30 from content delivery and approval). ATCHÉ Studio deducts its commission and pays the creator her share within an agreed window — recommend net-7 from when ATCHÉ receives brand payment, so creators aren't waiting on Studio's own collections timeline. This payout commitment should be written into the creator-facing agreement as a hard deadline, since on-time payment is core to ATCHÉ's trust positioning.

For AI likeness reuse, royalties should be tracked per use (which AI asset, which brand, which date) and paid out on a recurring cadence — monthly or quarterly — rather than per-transaction, to keep the admin overhead manageable while the volume is still low.

## Tracking

The Creator Pipeline and Brand Deals tabs in `ATCHE-Studio-Pilot-Tracking-Sheet.xlsx` are the natural home for this. Each brand deal row should carry: total deal value, commission tier applied, ATCHÉ's cut, creator payout, payment status (invoiced / received / paid out), and due dates for both legs. As deal volume grows this becomes the basis for a simple dashboard — total Studio revenue, total creator payouts, outstanding receivables/payables.

## Compensation Guardrail

Per the existing "pay first, perks second" principle: the commission structure above only works if the creator's base payout is real, on-time money. Access, exposure, or status framing should never be substituted for the creator's share — that's the differentiator that makes ATCHÉ's agency role trustworthy rather than extractive, and it's what separates this from the faceless UGC marketplaces (Billo, JoinBrands, Trend) referenced in the Phase 1 Pilot Kit.

## Worked Example

Using the Spicy Water tiers as a reference point: at the Standard tier ($1,800/mo) with 3 creators sharing the work, a 20% Studio commission means ATCHÉ retains $360/mo and the creators split $1,440/mo (roughly $480 each before any individual rate adjustments for role — photographer vs. talent vs. host). At the Full Partnership tier ($3,500/mo), the same 20% gives ATCHÉ $700/mo against $2,800/mo for creators. As deals move to retainer status and the commission steps down to 12-15%, ATCHÉ's per-deal margin shrinks but the predictability of recurring revenue — and the larger creator payout — strengthens the pitch for renewal and referrals.
