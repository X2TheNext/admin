How to Use Your Tools
A Real Guide for Monet · Updated June 2026
You don't need to memorize everything.
This guide walks you through exactly how to use every tool — from logging in to closing deals. Read it once, then come back when you hit something new.

Your System at a Glance

The Big Picture
Here's every tool you have and what each one is for.
✦ Dashboard
This is your home base. It's the first thing you open every morning. It has your follow-up queue (everyone who needs a message), your task checklist, a photography calendar, and a button to add new leads. Everything here is shared live with Xavier.
✦ Talent Tracker
The full database of every model, DJ, photographer, MUA, and creative ATCHÉ is working with. Xavier uses this for the deep work — ratings, scheduling, profile photos — but you can open it anytime to see the pipeline in Kanban view or look up details on anyone.
✦ Venue CRM
Every venue we're talking to or partnering with — from Focus Social Club to cold leads. Shows ATCHÉ Scores, revenue terms, pipeline stages, and contact info. Opens from your Hub or the Dashboard.
✦ Admin Guide (this doc's companion)
The task-by-task reference that tells you what to do. The guide you're reading now tells you how to do it. Bookmark both.
✦ The Playbook
The full strategy document. Read it once so you understand the business model, talent system, and venue framework. You don't need it daily — it's your context document.

Your Daily Routine

15–25 min · every morning
Open your Dashboard. Follow this sequence. The whole thing should take under 30 minutes once you're in rhythm.
1
Open your Dashboard
Go to atche-dashboard.vercel.app/monet-hub.html on your phone or laptop. Sign in with your email. Tap Dashboard.
Pro tip: Bookmark the Dashboard URL on your phone's home screen. One tap opens it like an app.
2
Scan the stats at the top
Look at the four numbers: Overdue, Due Today, Due This Week, and Events This Week. This tells you how heavy your day is in 2 seconds.
Red number ≠ panic. It just means someone slipped a day. Send the message today and they'll catch up.
3
Work through Overdue + Today cards first
Tap the 🔴 Overdue tab. Work through every card one by one, then switch to ⭐ Today and do the same. For each card:
① Read the person's name and stage so you know who you're reaching out to
② Tap Copy next to IG DM (or Text) — the message is now in your clipboard
③ Open Instagram or your text app, paste, send
④ Come back to the Dashboard and tap ✓ Sent
⑤ Pick when to follow up next (tap 3 days, 5 days, 1 week, or a custom date)
⑥ Card disappears — you're done with that person ✦
4
Snooze what you can't do right now
If you're not in the right headspace or the timing isn't right, just hit +3d on any card. It slides the follow-up 3 days forward and the card disappears. It'll come back on that date.
Don't overthink the templates. They're written to sound like you. The core message is good to go — you can add a sentence at the end if it feels right, but don't rewrite the whole thing.
5
Check the photography calendar
Tap the 📸 Calendar tab. Scan today's and tomorrow's shoots. For any confirmed event not already in your Google Calendar, tap the 📅 button to add it. It'll open Google Calendar pre-filled with all the event details.
If a shoot moved or got cancelled, text Xavier and update the notes in the Talent Tracker so it's recorded.
6
Log any new leads
Tap the + button (bottom right of your Dashboard). Anyone who expressed interest recently — a new model who applied, a venue owner Xavier mentioned, someone who slid into the DMs — add them now.
How to add a talent: Tap "Talent" → fill in name, category, Instagram handle, city, status, upload a photo if you have one, and add one line of notes about how you met. Hit Save. They now show up in Xavier's tracker too.

How to add a venue: Tap "Venue" → fill in name, contact person, Instagram, pipeline stage. Hit Save.
7
Cross-reference your Instagram DMs
Open Instagram. Scan your DMs for replies to messages you've sent from the Dashboard. Anyone who responded — go to their card in the Dashboard and update their stage.

If someone replied positively, change their stage to Interested or Meeting Set. If they said no, move them to Inactive.
Every card has an Open IG ↗ button that jumps straight to their profile. Use it instead of searching manually.
8
Check off your tasks
Tap the ✓ Tasks tab at the top of your Dashboard. You'll see a checklist of everything you're supposed to do today and this week. Tap each item to check it off. The progress bar fills up — aim for 100% by the end of the day.
The tasks reset every day (daily) and every week (weekly). Don't skip the weekly tasks — that's the work that moves ATCHÉ forward strategically, not just day-to-day.

Your Weekly Routine

30–45 min · pick one day
Pick a standing day — Monday or Friday works best. Protect it like a meeting. Do these in order.
1
Pipeline Review — open the Talent Tracker
From your Hub, open Talent Tracker. Tap Pipeline in the sidebar (or the bottom nav on mobile). This is the Kanban view — it shows every talent organized by stage.
What to do: Drag any card that changed status this week to the right column. Flag anyone who's been stuck in the same column for 2+ weeks — either schedule a follow-up or move them to Inactive. Don't let cards pile up in limbo.
2
Promote promising leads to full profiles
In the Talent Tracker, find anyone in "New Lead" or "Prospect" who's shown real interest. Open their profile by tapping their name. Add their photo, rating notes, and email if you have it — the email field unlocks the email template tab, which Xavier uses for the deeper outreach.
Even a one-sentence impression in the notes field ("Great energy, reliable, showed up on time") is gold for Xavier later when he's booking shoots.
3
Do an outreach batch
Back in your Dashboard, filter by Models and look at people in "New Lead" or "Contacted" stage. Pick 5–10 and send their messages. The templates are ready — just copy, paste, send, mark sent.
Use First Outreach for cold contacts. Follow-Up Check-in for people who haven't responded. Relationship Check-in for warm contacts who've gone quiet. The Dashboard picks the right template based on their pipeline stage automatically.
4
Sync the photography calendar
In your Dashboard, tap 📸 Calendar. Review every confirmed shoot for the next 2 weeks. For each one not already in your Google Calendar, tap 📅 to add it. Flag any scheduling conflicts to Xavier by text — don't try to resolve venue conflicts alone.
5
Open and scan the Venue CRM
From your Hub, open Venue CRM. Look at the Dashboard tab. Check the Pipeline Overview — are venues moving through the stages? Check Follow-Ups Due — are any venue contacts overdue for a message? Tap into any venue that needs attention.
Every venue card in your Dashboard now shows the contact name and neighborhood (e.g. "✦ Neb · H Street NE"). Use this to know who you're messaging at a glance.
6
Send Xavier your weekly recap
Drop him a quick text. Here's what he needs:
New leads this week: [number] Follow-ups sent: [number] Shoots confirmed: [list] Venue owners worth prioritizing: [names] Anything broken or missing in the tools: [notes]
Even two sentences is enough. This is how Xavier knows what's working and where to focus. Keep it consistent.

Feature-by-Feature Deep Dives

Reference
Read these when you encounter a feature for the first time, or when something doesn't make sense.
📱 How Templates Work
Every follow-up card has two templates: an IG DM version and a Text version. The Dashboard picks the right message automatically based on whether the person is a talent or venue — and what stage they're in. A "Prospecting" venue gets a different message than an "Active" partner. A "prospect" model gets a different message than a "booked" model.
Always use the template. They're written to sound natural and personal. Templates include the person's first name automatically. You never have to type it — just copy and paste.
🖼 How to Add Talent Photos
When you add a new talent lead (tap + → Talent), you'll see a Photo (optional) field below Status. Tap it and pick a photo from your phone. It gets saved to the shared database — so Xavier sees the face immediately.
Even a screenshot from their Instagram works. It's so much easier to remember who someone is when you can see their face. Once Xavier uploads a real profile photo from the Talent Tracker, it replaces whatever you uploaded.
⚡ How the Sync Indicator Works
At the top of your Dashboard, next to the queue count, there's a small green dot with the time your Dashboard last refreshed. If it turns red after a minute, that just means the data hasn't refreshed — your changes are still saved. When you reload or switch tabs, it'll turn green again.
If you ever wonder "is Xavier seeing my changes?", the answer is yes — everything saves to Supabase instantly when you mark someone as sent or add a new lead.
🏛 How Venue Cards Are Enriched
When you open a venue follow-up card, you'll now see the contact name and neighborhood right below the pipeline stage (e.g. "✦ Neb · H Street NE"). This tells you who you're messaging and where they are without having to open the full Venue CRM.
The contact name in the card header is the person the IG DM and Text templates address. So "Hey Neb" in the template matches what you see on the card.
🗓 How the Calendar Works
The 📸 Calendar tab shows every photography event Xavier has scheduled — paid shoots, donation gigs, meetings, venue pitches, and personal events — for the next 30 days. Each event card shows:
• Event type (Paid Shoot, Donation/Door Gig, Meeting, Venue Pitch, Personal)
• Time and venue (if filled in)
• Status badge (Scheduled, Confirmed, Done, Cancelled)
• 📅 button to add to your Google Calendar in one tap

This calendar is read-only — Xavier manages events from the Talent Tracker. Your job is to stay aware and keep your personal calendar in sync.
📋 How the Task Checklist Works
The ✓ Tasks tab has 7 daily tasks and 6 weekly tasks. Tap each one to check it off — they save to your phone's local storage. The progress bar at the top shows how much you've done. Tasks reset every day (daily) and every week (weekly).
Don't skip the weekly tasks just because they're not due today. The pipeline review, outreach batch, and Xavier recap are what turn day-to-day follow-ups into real business momentum. Block 30 minutes on your calendar for them.
🔍 How to Open the Talent Tracker or Venue CRM
From your Dashboard, tap ← Hub in the top bar. This takes you back to your Hub (monet-hub.html). From there, tap Talent Tracker or Venue CRM to open either tool. They open in the same tab, so to get back to your Dashboard, tap ← Hub in those tools and then Dashboard.
You can also bookmark the Talent Tracker and Venue CRM directly if you find yourself opening them often.

How to Get the Most Out of This System

Mindset
These are the things that separate "doing the tasks" from "moving the business forward."
✦ Protect your morning routine.
Open the Dashboard every single morning. Even if it's just 10 minutes. Consistency is everything. A week of missed follow-ups is hard to recover from — people move on fast.
✦ Don't over-personalize the templates.
The messages are written to sound like a real person — not a bot, not a corporation. They're casual but intentional. Adding a sentence at the end ("Btw I saw your post from last weekend — fire 🔥") is great. Rewriting the whole message is not.
✦ Speed matters more than perfection.
If someone responded, move them to the next stage immediately. A warm lead goes cold in 48 hours. The Dashboard makes this fast — one tap to mark sent, one tap to pick the follow-up date, done.
✦ Photos make everything easier.
When you add a new lead, upload a photo — even a screenshot of their Instagram profile. When you're scanning 20 cards in your queue, faces are 10x faster to process than names. Xavier can replace it with a proper photo later.
✦ Your notes are gold.
Every time you add a note — "she's friends with Zara," "he owns the spot on H Street," "great energy, needs coaching on posing" — you're building institutional knowledge that Xavier uses to book the right people for the right events. Even one sentence matters.
✦ When in doubt, text Xavier.
Something broken? Confused about a pipeline stage? Not sure if someone should be Active or On Hold? Text him. This system is built for both of you — he'd rather you ask than guess.

Pipeline Stages (Talent)
New Lead → Just found. Schedule first follow-up in 3–5 days.
Contacted → First message sent. Follow up in 5–7 days.
Interested → They replied. Move quickly — schedule a meeting.
Meeting Set → Call or in-person booked. Confirm day before.
Proposal Sent → ATCHÉ deck/rates sent. Follow up in 3–5 days.
Negotiating → Active discussion. Keep momentum.
Active / Signed → They're in. Switch to logistics.
On Hold → Interested but bad timing. Check back in 4–6 weeks.
Inactive → No response after 3+ attempts. Deprioritize.
Pipeline Stages (Venues)
Prospecting → Researching. Not yet contacted.
First Contact → Outreach sent. Waiting on reply.
Meeting Set → Meeting booked. Confirm.
Proposal Sent → Partnership proposal delivered.
Negotiating → Terms in discussion.
Signed → Deal locked. Transition to active.
Active → Ongoing partner.
