The Story

This isn't just an event.
It's our last dance with Everyday People.

For years, Everyday People has been the room. The block, the bar, the booth, the nights that turned into mornings. Now we're closing that chapter the only way that makes sense — loud, outside, together, on U Street, on Juneteenth.

ATCHÉ is throwing the block a party. Live music spilling into the street, the whole corridor lit up, every face that's ever danced in that room invited back for one more round. Freedom Day energy. Family reunion energy. Last dance energy.

Remembering Everyday People

Every Friday. Every birthday. Every "one more song."

This room has held so much of us — first dates, last calls, reunions, breakups, glow-ups, and every excuse to get dressed up on a Tuesday. The DJ booth that knew exactly when to drop it. The door staff who became family. The bar that always found you one more round.

Before we look forward, ATCHÉ wants to look back — with everyone who ever called this place home. Bring your old photos, your favorite memory, your people. We're building a wall for it.

"Everyday People gave us a room to be free in. The least we can do is give it the send-off it deserves."
