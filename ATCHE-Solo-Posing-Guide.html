Full Setup Notes

Reference, angle, and lighting cues for each pose.

01

The Approach

U Street energyOpener shot
Reference

U Street corridor vibe — neon bar signage and rowhouse silhouettes blurred behind her.

Setup

Shoot from roughly waist height, dead-on as she walks toward you. Wide aperture / portrait mode to blur the background. Catch her mid-stride, weight forward, chin level.

Why it works

Instant motion and confidence — strong first frame for any set.

02

The Lean

Shaw / Bloomingdale muralsRelaxed editorial
Reference

Any painted mural wall — Shaw, Bloomingdale, and Eaton-area alleys all work.

Setup

Shoulder against the wall, weight on the back leg, arms loose (not tightly crossed — that closes off the shot). Shoot at eye level or slightly below. Head turned toward camera, easy smile.

Why it works

Lets the mural's color do half the work — easy, confident, editorial.

03

Stoop Sit

Rowhouse stoopCandid / warm
Reference

Classic DC rowhouse front steps — porch light on, friends nearby (can be out of frame).

Setup

Seated, drink/cup optional. Shoot from slightly below looking up, or from across the street with more zoom. Talk to her — don't ask for a frozen pose, catch a real laugh.

Why it works

Candid warmth — feels like a real night out, not a shoot.

04

Brunch Cheers

Brunch patioDaytime / lifestyle
Reference

Outdoor brunch patio, any U Street/Adams Morgan spot — natural daylight, greenery or string lights in the background.

Setup

Seated at the table, glass raised toward the camera around chest/eye height. Shoot from just above table level. Stick to shade or golden-hour light — avoid harsh overhead sun.

Why it works

Covers the daytime brunch crowd — same energy as nightlife, just sunnier. Easy sell at weekend brunch tables.

05

Over the Shoulder

Marquee entranceStory shot
Reference

Glowing marquee or club entrance — Howard Theatre-style signage and warm lights.

Setup

Have her walk toward the entrance; call her name right as she reaches the door for the look-back. Shoot from behind/side with the marquee glow as backlight.

Why it works

Implies a story — she's heading somewhere, and so is the viewer.

06

The Adjust

Neon bokeh backdropBeauty detail
Reference

Any dark backdrop with ambient neon bokeh in the distance.

Setup

Tight three-quarter profile. Hand near ear/hair, not blocking the face. Rim light from one side — streetlight, neon sign, or phone flashlight in a pinch.

Why it works

Beauty/detail shot — jewelry and skin texture. Strong gallery filler.

07

Sidewalk Sunglasses

Daytime streetQuick / repeatable
Reference

Any DC sidewalk in daylight — rowhouses or storefronts in the background.

Setup

Standing, one hand lifting sunglasses slightly off the face, direct eye contact, easy smile. Shoot straight-on at eye level. Under 10 seconds — built for fast street approaches.

Why it works

The fastest pose in the set — ideal for daytime walkups when you've got seconds, not minutes.

08

The Twirl

Mural backdropHigh energy
Reference

Bold-color mural wall — gives the fabric something vibrant to pop against.

Setup

Crouch for a low angle. Have her spin and call "now" right as the fabric flares. Burst mode if your camera has it — pick the best fabric shape after.

Why it works

Movement plus color — usually the standout shot of the set.

09

Candid Laugh

Bar / loungeAuthentic
Reference

Any warm-lit bar or lounge interior, drink in hand.

Setup

Don't ask for a smile — get her talking or laughing with friends (even if they're cropped out). Medium close-up, slightly above eye level.

Why it works

Authenticity sells — every set needs one "real moment."

10

Power Stance

Bold mural, full bodyMain character
Reference

A bold, graphic DC mural with room for a full-body frame.

Setup

Full body, hands on hips, feet planted shoulder-width. Shoot straight-on at chest height. Check that the mural colors don't clash with the outfit.

Why it works

The "main character" shot — confidence and full fit visibility.

11

Glam Close-Up

Club interior lightingEditorial polish
Reference

Club interior with magenta/blue ambient lighting.

Setup

Tight beauty shot — jewelry, skin, makeup detail. Get close enough that the colored lighting wraps around her face. Shallow depth of field.

Why it works

The "magazine crop" shot — polish and detail.

12

Skyline Silhouette

Monument / skyline viewCloser shot
Reference

Rooftop, bridge, or waterfront with a DC skyline or monument view at night.

Setup

Expose for the skyline so she goes dark/silhouette. Find a rim light source (streetlight, building glow) to outline hair and shoulders.

Why it works

Ties the whole set back to place — strong closer for any DC shoot.
