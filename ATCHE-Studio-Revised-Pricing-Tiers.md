# ATCHÉ Studio: Revised Pricing Tiers

## The problem with the current ladder

The existing Spicy Water tiers — Starter $800/mo, Standard $1,800/mo, Full Partnership $3,500/mo — were built as a single ladder for one client. But "Standard" at $1,800/mo asks an independent restaurant to go from $0 (the free pilot) straight to a $21,600/year commitment with no track record. That's a big leap of faith for an owner who likely doesn't have a dedicated marketing line item at all. The fix isn't to lower your value — it's to recognize that independent local venues and bigger venues/chains/brands are different buyers who need different entry points.

## Segment-based ladder

**Segment A — Independent venues (restaurants, bars, lounges with one location, no marketing staff).** This is Spicy Water's segment. New entry tier:

*Pilot Partner — $0 (one-time):* The free proof-of-concept shoot you've already built. 3-4 Circle creators, one night, Spicy Water keeps all content. Purpose: prove the quality and get a "yes" to a paid relationship.

*Foundation — $450-600/mo:* 1-2 creators, one shoot per month, 4-6 pieces of content (reels/posts) delivered to the venue to post themselves. This is the natural next step after the free pilot — low enough that an owner can say yes without a budget conversation, but it's a real recurring contract and a real case study for you.

*Growth — $900-1,200/mo:* 2-3 creators, two shoots per month, 8-10 pieces of content, plus ATCHÉ handling posting/scheduling (not just delivery). This is roughly where the old "Starter $800" sat, nudged up slightly now that it's positioned as an upgrade rather than an opener.

**Segment B — Established venues, multi-location groups, local chains with a marketing budget.** This is where the old Standard/Full Partnership tiers belong:

*Standard — $1,800/mo:* 3-4 creators, weekly content, content calendar/strategy, light reporting (engagement, reach). Same deliverables as originally pitched — just repositioned for a buyer who can actually absorb this cost.

*Full Partnership — $3,500/mo:* Full roster rotation, multiple shoots/week, dedicated account management, campaign concepts tied to promotions/events, monthly reporting.

**Segment C — National brands / sponsorship deals.** This is revenue stream #2 already in the business model: $5k-25k one-off or campaign-based deals, 15-20% cut. No change needed here — it's a different motion entirely (pitch-based, not retainer).

## What this means for Spicy Water specifically

Spicy Water already got the free Pilot Partner shoot. The follow-up offer should be Foundation ($450-600/mo), not Standard ($1,800/mo). That's a much easier yes for an independent restaurant, gets you a paying client and a real testimonial faster, and gives you room to upsell to Growth once they see results — at which point $1,800 becomes a believable future ask rather than the opening price tag.

If there's been no response since June 11, consider a follow-up that explicitly offers the lower tier: "loved having you as our pilot partner — here's a simple monthly package at $X" reads very differently than re-sending the original three-tier deck.

## Net effect on your numbers

This doesn't lower your ceiling — Standard and Full Partnership stay where they are for the buyers who can support them. It adds a floor that independent venues can actually say yes to, which is what turns a one-time free shoot into a recurring revenue relationship and a portfolio of paying local clients you can point to when pitching the bigger venues and brand sponsorships later.
