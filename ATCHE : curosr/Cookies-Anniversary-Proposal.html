The short version

Cookies DC's first DC anniversary is a moment — and right now there's no plan for it yet. ATCHÉ wants to be the official media sponsor: we bring the people, the production, and the content engine that turns the night into something Cookies can point to for the next year. Free to test, same proof-of-concept structure that's already worked with Spicy Water and at Everyday People.

What ATCHÉ delivers as Official Media Sponsor

  • Full event coverage — professional photo + video, shot to look like a brand milestone, not a store event
  • A produced anniversary recap video — the kind of piece Cookies can post, run in-store on screens, and use for "one year on U Street" marketing for months after
  • Circle activation — ATCHÉ Circle members in the room as guests, content creators, and hosts, bringing a built-in crowd and built-in content from their own followings
  • Real-time social amplification — @atcheofficial covers before/during/after, tagging Cookies throughout
  • "Official Media Sponsor" branding on any event materials, signage, or digital promo Cookies puts out for the anniversary

What Cookies gets

  • A first DC anniversary that actually looks like a national brand's anniversary
  • A content library (video + photos) that outlasts the night
  • Built-in foot traffic and energy from ATCHÉ's community on a date they'd otherwise have to fill themselves
  • Zero production cost, zero planning lift — ATCHÉ handles the people and the output

Why now / why ATCHÉ

  • The corridor connection is already real. Cookies and Everyday People already have a relationship (NoCap discount for ATCHÉ's community) — this isn't a cold pitch, it's the next step of something already started.
  • Timing is on our side. A month of runway means this can actually be planned well, unlike a one-week scramble — worth using that time to make it look like the milestone it is.
  • This is the proof point. If this lands, it's the clearest possible demonstration that ATCHÉ can produce a brand moment at the level Cookies' national reputation deserves — which sets up a much bigger conversation afterward.

Long-term: what this seeds

If the anniversary lands well, the next conversation isn't "thanks, see you around" — it's a standing relationship:
  • Recurring content/activation partner — Cookies already runs community activations and talent contests; ATCHÉ's Circle becomes the go-to creative bench for those, ongoing rather than one-off
  • Brand-activation budget for corridor nights — Cookies' activation-table spend (already priced around $400-800 per night in past planning) could become a recurring line that helps fund other U Street corridor activations ATCHÉ organizes
  • Anchor brand for the U Street strategy — a national brand with real budget, on the same block as Everyday People and Spicy Water, is the kind of partner that makes the whole corridor pitch land harder with everyone else
  • A multi-location story — if this works in DC, it's a reference point for ATCHÉ in any other city where Cookies (or brands like it) operate — directly useful for the scalability conversation beyond DC
The first ask is small and free. The reason to do it well is everything that can follow it.
