Daily Admin Tasks

15–25 min · every day
Do these in order. The whole routine should take under 30 minutes once you're in the flow.
1 · Check the Follow-Up Queue
☐
Open the Follow-Up dashboard atche-followup.html — look at the stats row at the top: Overdue / Due Today / This Week.
☐
Work through every Overdue and Today card Tap the card to expand it. Pick a template (IG DM or Text). Hit Copy → paste into Instagram or texts → send.
Tip: Don't over-customize the templates. They're written to sound natural. Add a sentence at the end if you want — but the core message is good to go.
☐
After each message sent — hit ✓ Sent and set the next follow-up date Cards disappear from the queue once marked. They'll come back on the date you pick.
Not the right time? Hit +3d to snooze for 3 days. The card will come back automatically.
☐
Anyone who replied? Update their pipeline stage Open their card → change stage to "Interested," "Meeting Set," etc. so the tracker stays current.
2 · Review the Photography Calendar
☐
Tap the 📸 Calendar tab on your dashboard Scan today's and tomorrow's shoots. Confirm nothing changed.
☐
For any confirmed event not yet in your Google Calendar — tap 📅 to add it The button opens Google Calendar pre-filled with all the event details.
If a shoot moved or got cancelled, find the talent in the tracker and update their notes field so Xavier has context.
3 · Log New Leads
☐
Anyone who expressed interest recently? Add them now Hit the + button (bottom right of dashboard). Choose Venue or Talent → fill in name, IG handle, and stage.
☐
Add a quick note on how they found ATCHÉ or who referred them Even one line in the notes field helps future-you remember the context.
4 · Cross-Reference Instagram DMs
☐
Scan your IG DMs for replies to outreach you've sent Anyone who responded — go update their stage in the tracker right away.
Every talent card has an IG button that links straight to their profile. Use it instead of searching manually.

Weekly Admin Tasks

30–45 min · pick one day
Pick a standing day — Monday or Friday works best. Protect it like a meeting. This is the work that moves ATCHÉ forward strategically, not just day-to-day.
1 · Pipeline Review
☐
Open the Model Tracker → go to Kanban view Drag any cards that changed status this week to the right column.
☐
Flag anyone stuck in the same column 2+ weeks with no movement Either schedule a follow-up or move them to Inactive. Don't let cards pile up in limbo.
☐
Note any contacts who went from "Interested" to silent These are candidates for the Relationship Check-in email template.
2 · Add New Contacts to the Full Roster
☐
Promote any promising leads to a full profile in the tracker Add photo URL, category, IG handle, city. Fill in email if you have it — that unlocks the email template tab.
☐
Add rating notes or collab impressions from any meetings this week Even a one-sentence impression in the notes field is valuable for later.
3 · Weekly Outreach Batch
☐
Pick 5–10 people in "New Lead" or "Contacted" stage Open their profile → Email tab → choose the right template → customize → send.
Use "First Outreach" for cold contacts. "Follow-up Check-in" for no-responses. "Relationship Check-in" for warm contacts going quiet.
☐
After each send — hit ✓ Mark Contacted and schedule the next follow-up Building a consistent pipeline means nothing falls through the cracks.
4 · Sync Photography Calendar
☐
Review the Calendar tab for the next 2 weeks For every confirmed shoot not in your Google Calendar — tap 📅 to add it.
☐
Flag any scheduling conflicts to Xavier before they become a problem Text him the conflict. Don't try to resolve it alone if it involves a venue or partner.
5 · Send Xavier a Weekly Recap
☐
Drop him a quick text or note with: How many new leads added · How many follow-ups sent · Shoots confirmed or in discussion · Any venue owners worth prioritizing · Anything broken or missing in the tools
This is how Xavier knows what's working and where to focus. Even two sentences is enough — keep it consistent.

Pipeline Stage Guide

Reference for tagging contacts in the right stage. If you're not sure where someone belongs, put them in the earlier stage.
Stage What it means / what to do
New LeadJust found or just reached out for the first time. No response yet. Schedule a first follow-up in 3–5 days.
ContactedYou've sent the first message. Waiting on a reply. Follow up after 5–7 days of silence.
InterestedThey responded positively or asked for more info. Move quickly — schedule a meeting or send the ATCHÉ pitch.
Meeting SetCall or in-person meeting is booked. Confirm the day before. Add it to Google Calendar.
Proposal SentYou've sent the ATCHÉ deck or rate sheet. Follow up in 3–5 days if no response.
NegotiatingIn active discussion on rates, dates, or terms. Keep momentum — don't let it go quiet.
Active / SignedThey're in. Mark as Active. Switch from outreach to logistics coordination.
On HoldInterested but not the right time. Check back in 4–6 weeks. Set a follow-up reminder.
InactiveNo response after 3+ attempts, or they passed. Deprioritize — but don't delete. Seasons change.

Email Template Guide

All 6 templates are built into each talent's profile. Open their profile → Email tab → pick the right one → customize the name → send.
👋 First Outreach
When: First time contacting someone cold — model, DJ, photographer, or venue owner.
Don't customize too much. The simple, direct version converts better than a long pitch.
🔁 Follow-Up Check-in
When: They haven't responded after 5–7 days.
One follow-up maximum before moving on. Keep it short and low-pressure.
📸 Booking Confirmation
When: A shoot or event is confirmed and you need to send logistics.
Include date, time, location, and what they should bring or prepare.
✦ Collab Pitch
When: Pitching a venue partnership or creative collaboration.
Personalize the venue name and what specifically makes ATCHÉ the right fit for them.
💬 Relationship Check-in
When: Someone you've worked with going quiet. Keep the relationship warm.
Not a sales pitch. Just a genuine check-in. Makes a big difference long-term.
💵 Rate Inquiry
When: Asking about rates for a shoot, set, or service.
Keep it simple and professional. Don't over-explain ATCHÉ upfront — just ask the rate.
